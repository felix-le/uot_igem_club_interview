Cases give a sense of whether the number of new infections is growing and how quickly. However, there are a few days between each of the following: infection, the onset of symptoms, testing and test results, meaning that the numbers typically reflect new infections from several days and sometimes more than a week ago. Because the number of reported cases depends on how much testing is done and how targeted or widespread it is, epidemiologists consider deaths to be a better gauge of the actual number of infections and the progress of the epidemic. While it takes a couple of weeks for an infected person to be reported as a positive case, death occurs, on average, more than three weeks after a person has been infected.
